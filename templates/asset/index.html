What is an "asset"?

As the Metropolitan Area Planning Council (MAPC) moves forward in developing a vision for the area surrounding the potential Mystic Valley Parkway Green Line station, it is important that residents of surrounding communities are involved in identifying key community resources. MAPC will refer to these key resources as "assets".

Assets will be grouped under two categories for this mapping exercise: "Preservation" and "Change" . Preservation refers to assets which are of value to residents and should be preserved if the Green Line comes to Mystic Valley Parkway. Change refers to assets which offer opportunities for change and can have a positive impact for residents if the Green Line is extended.

MAPC is looking for specific examples of assets. These could include:

  • Buildings (i.e. U-Haul, 200 Boston Avenue, Whole Foods)
  • Parks or open space (i.e. Mystic River, Dilboy Field)
  • Transportation facilities (i.e. sidewalks, roadways, intersections)

We are not looking for general categories of assets such as housing or transportation. If you would like to see more housing or more commercial development or better sidewalks, please choose specific locations. All general comments can be submitted to MAPC at glx@mapc.org.

Why are we mapping assets?

The input provided through this website and at the public meeting on May 17th will be used by MAPC to develop different land use and development scenarios for the future around the potential Green Line station. By working with the public to map assets, MAPC will have a better understanding of what the public would like to see preserved in the area and what could be changed. This input will feed directly into the scenario modeling exercises that are planned for the upcoming fourth meeting for this project.

Add new asset

Instructions for mapping assets

  1. Click on Add New Asset under the map.
  2. Drag the red asset marker to the location of the asset you wish to map.
  3. You can zoom in or out or pan using your mouse or the controls on the left side of the map. You can also toggle between the road map and a satellite image.
  4. After you have placed your asset marker, please provide a title for the asset in the title box.
  5. Select whether the asset should be a preservation or change marker.
  6. Click the "Add!" button.
  7. If you wish to add a new comment to your asset, click the “add a new comment” link.
  8. If you wish to another asset, click on the "add another asset” link.

If the asset you want to map already exists, you can simply post a new comment and join the discussion by clicking on the marker instead of re-mapping the asset.
